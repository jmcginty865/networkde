A Digital Tool For Change

We have created a platform for volunteers and leaders to engage with each other in order to enact social change. By building a network we can help people in the communities they call home. Nobody knows the problems in a certain area better than the people who live there. By supplying volunteers, awareness, and funding in our network we can make Delaware a better place.

Connecting People with People

Organizations need people to make change and volunteers need organizations to get involved with. Anyone with an idea or problem can create their own "site" to reach out to people in the area. With the amount of information available and ways to reach out we can combine all efforts into one organized site.

Areas of Interest

Economic Justice

Economic justice is a component of social justice. It's a set of moral principles for building economic institutions, the ultimate goal of which is to create an opportunity for each person to create a sufficient material foundation upon which to have a dignified, productive, and creative life beyond economics.

Youth Engagement

Youth engagement is the result when young people are involved in responsible, challenging actions to create positive social change. This means involving youth in planning and in making decisions that affect themselves and others.

Electoral Politics

Electoral politics focuses on building a robust structure to support candidates for local, state and federal offices. By identifying, recruiting, and coordinating training and mentorship for candidates we will be able to enact change at a political level. This pillar will also establish a comprehensive fundraising system and support the development of responsive political parties.

Community Creativity

Solving problems can be complex, so looking at things in a different light can be beneficial. Art can inspire people to think bigger and think differently. That is why it is a vital asset for communities working to take on big problems or seize opportunities.

Racial Justice

Racial justice is a proactive reinforcement of policies, practices, attitudes and actions that produce equitable power, access, opportunities, treatment, impacts and outcomes for all. Most people view racism primarily as the result of individual action: personal prejudice or stereotyping, and intentional acts of discrimination by individuals. However, racism is also defined as a set of societal, cultural, and institutional beliefs and practices that subordinate and oppress one race for the benefit of another. However, a racial justice definition includes these beliefs and acts, but considers individual acts of prejudice only one dimension of racism.
